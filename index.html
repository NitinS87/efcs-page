    What is Student Processing Inventory?

    "Welcome to the Executive Functioning Competency Screener, an valuable tool for assessing and addressing executive functioning aptitude. This screener is designed to identify the likelihood of an executive functioning disorder and also provides insights into executive functioning skills across multiple domains. These include working memory, inhibitory control, cognitive flexibility, and higher-level executive functions. A detailed report offers explanations and remedial resources while a teacher dashboard enables group comparisons and analysis. Finally, for those that want to learn more about executive functioning, we offer a variety of free and informative resources.

    Our goal is to assist individuals, educators, clinicians, and coaches in comprehending and tackling the challenges related to executive functioning skills and determining if formal testing, cognitive remediation, or coaching is needed. By doing so, we aim to enhance overall functioning and success in various aspects of life, including education, work, and daily tasks.

    Join our community of individuals and professionals who have embraced the Executive Functioning Competency Screener. We look forward to supporting you."
    All the best, Dr. Erica Warren, Author

    Executive Functioning Explored

    Working Memory (WM)
    Working memory is a piece of executive functions that is responsible for:
    • Encoding information and knowledge
    • Memory storage and maintenance
    • Mental manipulation
    • Retrieval and updating information from memory
    Inhibitory Control (IC)
    Inhibitory Control is a piece of executive functions that is responsible for:
    • Interference control
    • Metacognition
    • Inhibition of responses
    • Emotional regulation
    Cognitive Flexibility (CF)
    Cognitive Flexibility is a piece of executive functions that is responsible for:
    • Mental shifting
    • Adaptation
    • Accommodating different perspectives
    • Employing different strategies or approaches
    Higher Level EF
    Higher-level executive functions unites the tools of WM, IC, and CF and is responsible for:
    • Strategic planning and organizing
    • Time management and scheduling
    • Decision making and problem solving
